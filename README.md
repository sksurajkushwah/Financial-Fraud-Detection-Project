🏦 Financial-Fraud-Detection-Project

📌 Problem Statement
Financial institutions rely on credit scores to evaluate a customer's creditworthiness before approving loans or credit products. However, credit scores are influenced by multiple financial and behavioral factors such as income level, outstanding debt, credit utilization, payment behavior, and loan history.
Understanding how these factors interact is essential for identifying potential financial risks and improving decision-making processes. Additionally, detecting unusual patterns in customer behavior is critical for identifying anomalies that may indicate fraudulent activities or high-risk profiles.
Despite the availability of data, extracting meaningful insights for risk assessment and fraud detection remains a challenge.
